Spartacus Project

The name of this project, Spartacus, takes inspiration from a slave with the same name who lived in Rome around 100 BC.
This Thracian, turned gladiator, orchestrated one of the most fascinating rebellions of his time. 1,700 years after his death, the Spartacus rebellion inspired another slave to lead a revolt, Toussaint Louverture. A natural born military genius, Louverture organized the first successful slave revolt in Haiti, setting the stage for the first black republic.
